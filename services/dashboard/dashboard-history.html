Launched in 2009 under the Obama administration, Data.gov was built to consolidate access to federal datasets and spark civic tech innovation. At its peak, it linked to over 250,000 datasets. But maintenance and decentralization have since reduced its clarity and relevance.

Many agencies lack APIs or robust metadata. CDC data, for example, lives in several separate platforms. Inkwell monitors:

  • Socrata: disease surveillance, maternal health, mortality, and more
  • MMWR: peer-reviewed data summaries, hard to quantify
  • VAERS: vaccine safety reports with limited metadata

Dashboard spikes reveal changes in data flow: metadata updates, compressed releases, or delayed uploads. These patterns show how fragile and fragmented federal data infrastructure can be.
